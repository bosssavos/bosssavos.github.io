GAMES

Games We Offer

Various games with different genres you can play!

Action/Adventure

Action/Adventure games are a genre of video games that combine elements of both action and adventure games. They typically feature fast-paced, challenging gameplay, as well as a narrative-driven story, puzzle-solving, exploration, and character development. These games often feature a main character who must navigate various obstacles and enemies to complete quests and achieve a goal. Examples include "Uncharted," "Tomb Raider," and "The Legend of Zelda."


First-Person Shooter (FPS)

First-Person Shooter (FPS) games are a genre of video games where the player experiences the game through the eyes of the main character and typically involves shooting action. Players use weapons to defeat enemies and advance through levels. FPS games can be played in single-player mode, with a storyline and set of objectives to complete, or in multiplayer mode, where players can compete against each other. The genre often features fast-paced and intense gameplay, with an emphasis on quick reflexes and accuracy. Examples include "Call of Duty," "DOOM," and "Half-Life."

Multiplayer/Co-op

Multiplayer/Co-op games are video games that allow players to play together with or against each other. They can be played on a single device or online over the internet. In cooperative games, players work together to complete a common goal or objectives, while in competitive games, players compete against each other. Multiplayer/Co-op games can range from simple party games to complex strategy games, and they can be enjoyed by players of all ages and skill levels. Examples include "Overwatch," "Minecraft," and "Rainbow Six Siege."


Racing

Racing games are a genre of video games where players compete in races using vehicles, such as cars, motorcycles, boats, or airplanes. The goal of these games is to be the first to cross the finish line or complete a set number of laps. Racing games can be played in a variety of environments, such as on city streets, racetracks, off-road terrain, or in futuristic worlds. They can also feature various types of races, such as circuit, drag, or rally. Racing games can be played in single-player mode or multiplayer mode, and often feature realistic physics and vehicle handling. Examples include "Forza Horizon," "Gran Turismo," and "Need for Speed."

Strategy/Puzzle

Strategy/Puzzle games are a genre of video games that require players to use critical thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making skills to progress. These games often feature turn-based gameplay, where players take turns making moves, or real-time gameplay, where players make moves simultaneously. Strategy games often have a military or geopolitical focus, requiring players to manage resources, build armies, and engage in battles. Puzzle games, on the other hand, usually require players to solve increasingly difficult challenges using logic and spatial reasoning. Examples of strategy games include "Civilization," "Chess," and "StarCraft." Examples of puzzle games include "Tetris," "Portal," and "The Room."


Horror

Horror games are a genre of video games that aim to scare and thrill the player. They often feature survival elements, where players must avoid dangerous creatures or escape from a haunted location. The games typically have a dark and suspenseful atmosphere, with jump scares, creepy sound effects, and unsettling environments. Players may also face moral dilemmas and difficult choices as they progress through the game. Horror games can be played in single-player or multiplayer mode, and can range from psychological horror to supernatural or action-horror. Examples include "Outlast," "Resident Evil," and "Dead Space."
